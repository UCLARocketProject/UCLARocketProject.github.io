Workshop 1 - Foundations of Digital Electronics

The first Workshop focused on the Foundations of Digital Electronics, covering the topics of resistance (voltage divider), maximum power trasfer theorem, capacitance in terms of a short-lasting power source, transistors (as a simple electrical switch, allowing the current to pass through or not).

Optional homework: If you didn't have enough after the first workshop consider the following design problem:

P1.1 You have a microcontroller running at 3.3V, but a power supply delivering 9V. How would you reduce the voltage down in the best possible way. (Rember: A microcontroller can be modeled as a resistive load, but as a hugely variable one!)

P1.2 Now what if you only have a 1.5V and the same 3.3V microcontroller?

P1.3 Build a break-wire circuit which energizes an output line when the break-wire is broken.

P1.4 Build the same break-wire circuit which connects the output line to ground (GND) when break-wire is broken.

P1.5 Design the simplest switch circuit (involving a relay and a MOSFET at a minimum). Don't forget to define the line state (pull-up, pull-down).

P1.6 Build an inverter circuit.

P1.7 Design a level shifting circuit for digital communication.
