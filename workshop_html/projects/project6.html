Some technical payload information surely no one wants to read: The payload will consist of a full sensor package allowing to fully determine a 3D position and attitude of a rocket. The used sensors will likely include a pair of an accelerometer and a gyroscope, with an aid of a digital compass, to determined the attitude of the rocket in 3D rotation frame with a reference to a fixed point. The position will have to include 3D coordinates and so the use of a gps for plane positioning and a pressure sensor for altitude sensing appears to be reasonable. The payload must be equipped with a local storage device for data logging and means of transmitting acquired data to ground. Transmission presents a data rate bottleneck and will likely be between 10-100Hz depending on the choice of radio and trade-off between transmission robustness. Electrical characteristics should involve operation at a maximum of 5V to allow for the use of standardized power source (likely a double lithium-ion cell).
